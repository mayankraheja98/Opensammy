Design Review

Stage 1

Objective: Support ad hoc reviews of software design to ensure baseline mitigations for known risks.
Activities: A.Identify software attack surface
B.Analyze design against known security requirements

Question 1: Do project teams document the attack perimeter of software designs?

Mark Yes if, >50% of projects with updated attack surface analysis in past 12 months

Question 2: Do project teams check software designs against known security risks?

Mark Yes if, >50% of projects with updated security requirements design-level analysis in past 12 months

Stage 2

Objective: Offer assessment services to review software design against comprehensive best practices for security
Activities: A.Inspect for complete provision of security mechanisms
B.Deploy design review service for project teams

Question 1: Do most project teams specifically analyze design elements for security mechanisms?

Mark Yes if, >75% of projects undergoing design review in the past 12 months

Question 2: Are most project stakeholders aware of how to obtain a formal design review?

Mark Yes if, >80% of stakeholders briefed on status of review requests in the past six months

Stage 3

Objective: Require assessments and validate artifacts to develop detailed understanding of protection mechanisms.
Activities: A.Develop data-flow diagrams for sensitive resources
B.Establish release gates for design review

Question 1: Does the design review process incorporate detailed data-level analysis?

Mark Yes if, >80% of projects with updated dataflow diagrams in the past six months

Question 2: Does routine project audit require a baseline for design review results?

Mark Yes if, >75% of projects passing design review audit in the past six months
