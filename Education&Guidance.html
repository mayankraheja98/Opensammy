Education & Guidance

Stage 1

Objective:Offer development staff access to resources around the topics of secure programming and deployment.
Activities: A.Conduct technical security awareness training
B.Build and maintain technical guidelines

Question 1: Have most developers been given highlevel security awareness training?

Mark Yes if, >50% development staff briefed on security issues within the past year AND >75% senior development/ architect staff briefed on security issues within the past year

Question 2: Does each project team have access to secure development best practices and guidance?

Mark Yes if, Launched technical guidance program within three months of first training

Stage 2

Objective:Educate all personnel in the software life-cycle with role-specific guidance on secure development.
Activities: A.Conduct role-specific application security training
B.Utilize security coaches to enhance project teams

Question 1: Are most roles in the development process given role-specific training and guidance?

Mark Yes if, >60% development staff trained within the past year AND >50% management/analyst staff trained within the past year AND >80% senior development/architect staff trained within the past year

Question 2: Are most stakeholders able to pull in security coaches for use on projects?

Mark Yes if, >3.0 Likert Scale on usefulness of training courses

Stage 3

Objective:Mandate comprehensive security training and certify personnel for baseline knowledge.
Activities: A.Create formal application security support portal
B.Establish role-based examination/certification

Question 1: Is security-related guidance centrally controlled and consistently distributed throughout the organization?

Mark Yes if, >80% staff certified within the past year

Question 2: Are most people tested to ensure a baseline skillset for secure development practices?

Mark Yes if, >80% staff certified within the past year
